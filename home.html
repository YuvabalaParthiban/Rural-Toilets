IMPORTANCE OF SANITATION AND TOILETS


-> Adequate sanitation and toilets are basic necessities that ensure and promote the health of people in developing countries.
->The importance of sanitation and toilets lies in helping reduce the spread of diseases.
-> Sanitation systems aim to protect health by providing and promoting a clean environment.
->Today, 2.4 billion people are struggling to stay healthy, keep their children alive, and work their way to a better future – all for the want of a toilet. Let’s change that.


-> Developing countries face challenges in accessing sanitation and hygiene care.
-> Over one million deaths occur due to diseases transmitted via poor sanitation and lack of hygiene.
-> Hand-washing with soap after using the toilet is one of the most effective and inexpensive ways to prevent diarrhoeal diseases and pneumonia.
-> This simple act can reduce the risk of diarrhoeal disease by up to 47 per cent, but only if it’s done consistently.


-> Toilets are crucial for the healthy development of people.
-> In South Asia alone, 1 billion people lack access to improved sanitation and 675 million practice open defaecation, more than any other region in the world.
-> Open defaecation is a leading cause of diarrhoeal death, says the WHO. The diarrhoea death stands at around 6,000 a day, mostly young children.
-> Everyone must use toilets so that they can avoid the health issues caused by open defaecation.
